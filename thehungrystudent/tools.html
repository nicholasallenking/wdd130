The Right Tools

Many students, new and experienced, are seen with only the most basic of cooking tools like a frying pan, spatula, and a sauce pot. While many parents of prospective students may help them prepare for their careers in further education, there are still much for these fresh cooks need to know before they can truly make their kitchens tue cooking spaces. While there are many different situations for students, some have full cooking spaces while others may only have a corner in their dorms. These situations all will determine what students will consider adding to their arsenal of appliances and tools, which this site will seek to provide for each kind of student. Ranging form affordable and compact, the appliances and tools that will be featured here wil provide insights for students who may want to improve their cooking quality while not breaking the bank. The appliances featured here will provide differing options for different budgets, though some appliances may be good to consider as decent investments for students down the line.

Low Cost

Starting with the Low Budget options, a great starting appliance would be a Toaster Oven. Many current models give ample provide decent options for
for most meals while not taking too much space, while also having additional features, like air frying. Just as you should pay attention to your
budget, consider your available space for storage.

Mid-to-High Cost

A more mid-to-higher expensive appliance that is suggested is a multi-cooker. I say mid-to-higher because the cost depends on which brand
or model you choose. One of the more popular brads out there is the Instant Pot, which gives their users many options to cook with, including
pressure-cooking, slow-cooking, sauteing, and prebuilt options for cooking different kinds of foods, like poultry or rice. Some models,
if you're willing to pay the price, can include features such as sous vide or even air frying with additional accessories.
Remember, keep track of your space. Don't get an appliance you can't store. Luckily, many of these kinds of appliances are fairly compact.
